    About these Variables

    All these intervention levers are taken directly from the Committee on Accelerating Progress in Obesity Prevention recommendations, strategies and action steps. [IOM (Institute of Medicine). 2012. Accelerating Progress in Obesity Prevention: Solving the Weight of the Nation. Washington, DC: The National Academies Press.]

    The simulation interface will consist of organized variables and input options that users have access to. The input options are primarily checkboxes or buttons with "low/medium/high" or the equivalent. All variables are organized into three categories called •contexts•: •physical activity context•, •diet behavior context•, and •individual-level behavior context•.
